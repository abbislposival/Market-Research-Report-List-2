<p><p>The dental X-ray market is primarily categorized into intraoral and extraoral X-rays. Intraoral X-rays involve placing the film or sensor inside the patient's mouth, capturing detailed images of individual teeth and their surrounding structures. They are essential for diagnosing cavities, assessing tooth roots, and examining bone health. Conversely, extraoral X-rays involve capturing images from outside the mouth, such as panoramic X-rays that provide a broader view of the jaw and teeth. Both types are crucial for accurate dental diagnosis and treatment planning.</p></p>
